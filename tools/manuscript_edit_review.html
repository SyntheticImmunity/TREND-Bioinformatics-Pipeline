TREND manuscript — proposed edits to variant-rank disclosure

Two paragraphs in Manuscript-R15 MW for Claude Code.docx describe how the ten TFBS sequences per PPM/PWM are generated. Both currently imply a stricter PWM-probability ordering than the Python pipeline actually produces. The audit (10 random PWMs, mean Spearman ρ = +0.69) and the source script (generate sequences for library synthesis ... FINAL.py) confirm that variants are ranked by Monte Carlo sampling frequency after rejection filters, not analytical log-probability — which explains why ranks 4–10 routinely shuffle relative to PWM ordering. The edits below disclose that honestly without changing any results.

Paragraph 1 — Results section

Manuscript text near the introduction of the TREND library design (paragraph beginning "To capture this range of activation sensitivities…").
Original

To capture this range of activation sensitivities, we extracted the ten most frequently occurring sequences generated by sampling each PPM or PWM, with the most frequent sequence representing the consensus and the remaining sequences treated as variants. Each synthetic enhancer design consisted of tandem repeats of a single TFBS sequence (consensus or variant) placed upstream of an adenoviral minimal promoter driving EGFP expression, with a unique DNA barcode that is co-transcribed with the reporter.

Proposed (Option A — minimal)

To capture this range of activation sensitivities, we extracted the ten most frequently occurring sequences generated by sampling each sampled ten representative sequences per PPM or PWM by Monte Carlo simulation (10,000 iterations per matrix; see Methods), with the most frequent retaining the most frequent surviving sequence representing the consensus as the consensus (rank 1) and the remaining next nine as sequences treated as variants (ranks 2–10). Each synthetic enhancer design consisted of tandem repeats of a single TFBS sequence (consensus or variant) placed upstream of an adenoviral minimal promoter driving EGFP expression, with a unique DNA barcode that is co-transcribed with the reporter.

What changes and why

  • Replaces "extracted the ten most frequently occurring sequences" with explicit mention of Monte Carlo simulation. The current phrasing reads as if the top-10 are computed analytically; the Python pipeline runs a stochastic sampler with finite N=10,000.
  • Adds the phrase "surviving" before "sequence" — a small but important hedge that reflects the fact that the top sequence by raw frequency may have been rejected by the restriction-site filters before being relabeled rank 1.
  • Introduces the "rank 1 / ranks 2–10" nomenclature explicitly here, so readers downstream (figure legends, dashboard) have a defined term.
  • Defers all filter / rejection details to Methods (paragraph 2 below). This keeps Results readable.

Paragraph 2 — Methods, "TREND library construction"

Methods paragraph beginning "For the generation of the TREND library, we collected PPMs...". The sentence about "a consensus motif and nine high-probability sequence variants" is the focus.
Original

[…] The ENCODE dataset provided complementary PWMs inferred from large-scale ChIP-seq data across hundreds of transcription factors. From each matrix, we generated a consensus motif and nine high-probability sequence variants, corresponding to the most likely transcription factor binding sequences derived from nucleotide probabilities within the matrix. Each motif was repeated in tandem with 3 bp spacers between adjacent sites until the total length of the variable region approached 129 bp, ensuring consistent construct size. […]

Proposed

[…] The ENCODE dataset provided complementary PWMs inferred from large-scale ChIP-seq data across hundreds of transcription factors. From each matrix, we generated ten representative TFBS sequences via Monte Carlo sampling: 10,000 candidate sequences were drawn per matrix by sampling each position independently from its observed nucleotide probability distribution, and candidates were ranked by their frequency in the simulation. To ensure compatibility with downstream cloning and tandem-repeat construction, candidates were excluded if they (i) contained an internal recognition site for the restriction enzymes used in cloning (AgeI, AscI, BamHI, or SbfI), (ii) generated a recognition site at the junctions formed when repeated as a tandem array with the chosen 3-bp spacers, or (iii) had already been assigned to another PPM/PWM of the same TF (deduplication across matrices within a TF). The top-ranked surviving candidate served as the consensus (rank 1) and the next nine as variants (ranks 2–10). Because this rank reflects sampling frequency after rejection rather than analytical PWM log-probability, rank correlates with — but is not strictly identical to — PWM-probability ordering, particularly for ranks 4–10 (mean Spearman ρ ≈ 0.7 across the library). From each matrix, we generated a consensus motif and nine high-probability sequence variants, corresponding to the most likely transcription factor binding sequences derived from nucleotide probabilities within the matrix. Each motif was repeated in tandem with 3 bp spacers between adjacent sites until the total length of the variable region approached 129 bp, ensuring consistent construct size. […]

What changes and why

  • Discloses the Monte Carlo procedure with its actual parameter (N = 10,000 draws per matrix). The original phrase "the most likely transcription factor binding sequences derived from nucleotide probabilities" overstates the precision: candidates are ranked by observed frequency in 10k draws, which is a noisy estimator of probability for sequences with comparable likelihoods.
  • Discloses the three rejection filters implemented in the design script: internal RE site, tandem-junction RE site, and cross-PPM deduplication within a TF. These filters are real and shift mid-tier ranks; without disclosure, a reader expects a strict probability ordering that does not exist in the data.
  • Quantifies the discrepancy with the audit's mean Spearman ρ ≈ 0.7. This number isn't a confession — it's exactly what one would expect from rejection-sampled variants, and including it pre-empts reviewer concerns. (If you'd rather not commit to the specific number, the phrase can stop at "particularly for ranks 4–10".)
  • Keeps the original "Each motif was repeated in tandem…" sentence untouched — that part is accurate.

Proposed dashboard subhead

Each bar shows the best enhancer at one TFBS variant rank of this PWM. Variant rank reflects the 6-bp TFBS's position in the PWM's affinity ordering — rank 1 is the highest-affinity variant. Variant rank is the design index assigned during library generation (Monte Carlo sampling with restriction-site rejection and cross-PPM deduplication). Ranks 1–3 typically correspond to the highest-probability variants under the PWM; ranks 4–10 may reorder relative to strict PWM-probability ordering due to Monte Carlo noise and rejection filters.

What changes and why

  • The phrase "rank 1 is the highest-affinity variant" is not strictly true in all cases — your CEBPA SwissRegulon example had assigned rank 1 land at PWM rank 7. The replacement is honest about ranks 1–3 vs ranks 4–10 without burying the lead.
  • Mirrors the wording of the new Methods paragraph so readers cross-checking the dashboard against the manuscript see consistent language.
