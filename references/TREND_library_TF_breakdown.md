## Definition of Each Subset

### 695 — Direct Lambert matches

TFs whose gene symbol matches a Lambert entry exactly. These are the 695 that get distributed across 49 DBD families in Figure 1B.

Examples: MYC, PAX8, E2F7, FOS, BACH1, ZBTB33, FOSL1, NFKB1, NFAT5

### 248 — Not in Lambert census

Proteins shown to bind DNA in unbiased protein-DNA interactome screens (e.g., Hu et al. 2009 — the protein microarray dataset cited as reference 19 in the manuscript) but not classified as canonical TFs by Lambert. Many are RNA-binding proteins or accessory factors with non-canonical DNA-binding activity.

Sample: A1CF, ABCF2, ACO1, ADARB1, AFP, AGAP2, AGGF1, ANXA1, ANXA11, AP3, APEX2, ARFGAP1, ARHGEF12, ASAP3, ASPSCR1...

### 91 — Lambert entries flagged as non-TFs

These ARE in Lambert but are explicitly flagged as not being sequence-specific transcription factors:

- **75** classified as "Unlikely to be sequence-specific TF"
- **16** classified as "ssDNA/RNA binding"

## Notes for Manuscript Wording

The current manuscript sentence:

> *"The remaining 373 targets include 34 confirmed TFs matched to the Lambert census via gene synonyms, 248 proteins not present in the Lambert census..., and 91 entries classified as non-TFs."*

This reads slightly awkwardly because the 34 "confirmed TFs" are grouped with the "remaining 373" as if they weren't really TFs — when in fact they should count toward the 729 total.

**Suggested rewording:**

> *"Of the remaining 373 targets, 34 are confirmed sequence-specific TFs matched to Lambert via gene synonyms (bringing the total confirmed TF count to 729), 248 are proteins not present in the Lambert census (derived from protein-DNA interaction screens), and 91 are Lambert entries not classified as sequence-specific TFs (75 unlikely to be sequence-specific TFs and 16 ssDNA/RNA-binding proteins)."*

This makes the arithmetic transparent: 695 + 34 = 729 confirmed TFs, and 729 + 248 + 91 = 1,068.

## Cross-Reference Coverage Summary

| Reference dataset | Library TFs matched | Total in dataset | Coverage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lambert (sequence-specific TFs) | 729 | 1,639 | 44.5% |
| Reddy (cancer master TFs) | 215 | 273 | 78.8% |
| D'Alessio (tissue identity TFs, top-10) | 363 | 503 | 72.2% |

**Note on the Reddy and D'Alessio numbers:** Your manuscript currently states 74% and 71% respectively. The new numbers (78.8% and 72.2%) reflect the addition of alias-based matching. The differences:

- **Reddy:** 74% (203/274) → 78.8% (215/273) — gains 12 TFs via alias matching (e.g., EVI1→MECOM, which is a key MTF in multiple tumor types)
- **D'Alessio:** 71% (357/503) → 72.2% (363/503) — gains 6 TFs via alias matching

Both updates reflect more biologically accurate coverage. The conservative direct-match-only numbers can be retained if preferred — both are defensible, but updating to alias-resolved numbers is more rigorous.
